Is Tesla Committing Fraud?

4 April 2019
2 minute read

Tesla is consistently amazing everyone with their stream of launches and ability to keep their stock price afloat with reasonable if volatile sales numbers. But is full self driving realistic? I’ll begin with a full disclaimer that I’ve contributed non-dilutive capital to Tesla and I’m also a shareholder. I’ll continue to be a shareholder (as part of my risk portfolio), and plan on buying future Tesla products.

I’m a believer in almost everything Elon Musk has developed or begun to develop, including controversial technologies like SpaceX’s satellite Internet1 and Boring Company tunnels (despite the lackluster launch). There is one technology, however, which is being sold by Tesla that is dubious which is Full Self Driving.

My background is in cognitive science and computer science, and I’ve built hardware and software for ambulatory and wheeled robots, though I’m not an expert in self driving cars. Even so, experts like (Google guy) and Rodney Brooks who cofounded iRobot weigh heavily on the matter. I’ve interacted with Google’s self-driving cars on bike in the streets of Mountain View and that sort of thing is a real eye-opener – I wouldn’t want to be in a self-driving car in a dangerous part of the city.
